Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

To determine if your helmet is too old, check for cracks, dents, or worn-out padding, as these are signs of degradation. Most helmets should be replaced every five years, as materials can weaken with age, reducing protection.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

After storage, inspect your bike for rust, tire pressure, brake function, and chain lubrication. Test each component carefully to ensure safety, as long-term storage can impact performance and reliability.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding, follow traffic signals, stay in designated lanes, wear visible clothing, use hand signals, and always wear a helmet to ensure your safety.
